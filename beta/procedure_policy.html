Procedure & Policy

Procedure

  1. Invitations of the onset of the Placements with the tentative itinerary of the placement process along with the Placement brochure are sent to the company
  2. After a company expresses interest for participation, the company is sent a login ID & password which they can use to log on to the Placement Automation System (PAS)
  3. The Placement Automation System(PAS) is an interactive portal that lets companies post a job opportunity & view the profiles of students interested in the Job Profile
  4. Based upon mutual agreement, Pre-Placement Talks are scheduled
  5. Meanwhile, students can access the company profile and prepare themselves for the PPT
  6. After the Pre-Placement Talk, students submit their resumes according to the deadline set by the company on the PAS
  7. Companies are informed about their slots for the placement season which starts from December 1
  8. Slotting is done subject to following parameters
    • Student Preferences
    • Work profile
    • Compensation package
    • Career Prospects
    • Student Intake
  9. Any pre-requisite of online/offline tests are conducted
  10. Based upon student â€™ profiles and/or test performances, short-list of students is provided either before the commencement of placement session or at the time of placement session
  11. Shortlisted candidates then appear for the Placement Process of the company
  12. Company submits a list of finally selected students at the end of the process on the same day
  13. A student may get multiple offers at the end of a day, in such a case he/she is allowed to choose between one of them.
  14. Once a student has been offered a job, he/she is out of the placement process. This is in compliance with our One Student, One Job Policy
  15. The complete process is governed by SPO's placement policy which is shared with the companies at the start of the process.

Placement Policy 2013-14

  • The Students' Placement Office follows a strict "One Student, One Job Policy". Once a student is offered a job, he/she is out of the placement process
  • On completion of the recruitment procedure, the company needs to submit the final list of offers in a closed envelope to the placement office. The offers will be opened at the end of the slot* when all companies finish their process
  • No "On the Spot" offers should be made nor any cue can be given to any of the candidates in between the placement of
  • In case a candidate receives multiple offers, the candidate has to make a choice at the end of the slot.The companies will be intimated about the final choice of the candidate next slot in the morning.
  • Since there is a high probability of multiple offers, we strongly recommend the companies to furnish a waiting list to the placement office along with the final offers. In case a student who is extended multiple offers chooses the other company, the first candidate in the waiting list moves up in the final offer list. The waitlist will not be disclosed to the students and will be released on a per student basis
  • Companies are advised to be in touch with the Placement volunteers and staff at all times. They should not entertain any direct contact from the students appearing in the placement process. In case of such a situation, the company must bring this immediately to the notice of the placement office
    * The slot allotted for the selection procedure by the placement office to the company is of 8 hrs. Although the companies are allowed to conduct tests on the previous day to shortlist the candidates
